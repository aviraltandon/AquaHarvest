Our Mission

Advancing SDG 6 — improving availability of water.

Condensing atmospheric humidity into water — though raw output still needs basic filtering/UV before it's drinkable. This can be used with solar pannels, in several industries requiring water such as AI, Agriculture etc. Also doubling as an off-grid irrigation source and cutting reliance on groundwater and water transport.

How it works

The Peltier effect — turning electricity into a cold surface

A Peltier module (also called a TEC, thermoelectric cooler) is a solid-state device made of paired p-type and n-type semiconductor blocks sandwiched between two ceramic plates. When DC current is driven through the junctions, charge carriers absorb heat energy on one side to cross into the next semiconductor and release it on the other — a phenomenon called the Peltier effect. The net result: one face of the module gets cold, the other gets hot, with no moving parts and no refrigerant.

In AquaHarvest, the cold face is pushed below the surrounding air's dew point — the temperature at which air can no longer hold all its water vapor. Once the plate is colder than the dew point, moisture in the air condenses on it directly, the same way condensation forms on a cold glass on a humid day. The condensed droplets are collected and channeled into a reservoir, while the hot face (fitted with a heatsink and fan) dumps the extracted heat back into the room. That's why the dashboard tracks dew point live: it's the threshold the cold plate has to beat for water to actually form.
